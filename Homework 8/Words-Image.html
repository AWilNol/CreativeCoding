Littles

By AWilNol

Outside they wait, biding time borrowed from homework undone.
They bounce and squeal, maybe ride their wheels, tha-thacking on abused sidewalks, closer now to breaking.
Every day they creep from cover. Littles. Cloudy. Sun-shiny. Snowy or rainy.
Their voices flash lightning. Shrieks to ring bells.
Striking to scare, yet raising tendrils ensuring no weakness.
No Blood, they come in. Blood it is quicker.
Tromp-tromping havoc over heads and or underfoot. No thought to little for another.
Littles. Call them what you will. Twerps. Punks. Kids.
I actually love mine, but here's your story! The End!
